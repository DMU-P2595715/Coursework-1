SYNOPSIS

The Gentlemen is a 2019 British-American comedy film which was written, directed and produduced by Guy Ritchie. The film stars Matthew McConaughey, Charlie Hunnam, Michelle Dockery, Jeremy Strong, Eddie Marsan, Colin Farrell and Hugh Grant. It follows the narrative of an American Kingpin who moved to England looking to sell his marijuana business, however this sets of a chain of blackmail and schemes in order to undermine him.

Mickey Pearson (Mathew McConaughey) is an American expatriate who became rich by building a highly profitable marijuana empire in London. When word gets out that he's looking to cash out of the business, it soon triggers an array of plots and schemes, including bribery and blackmail from shady characters who want to steal his domain. Born in poverty in the US, Pearson won a scholarship to Oxford University in England, where he began selling marijuana to students before inevitably dropping out and building his criminal empire. After making millions he now plans to sell hsi business to an American billianaire Mathew Berger for $400m so that he can retire from his life of crime and violence with his wife Rosalind. Pearson is also approached by Dry Eye, an underboss of Chinease gangster Lord George. Dry Eye offers to buy Pearson's business however he is refused. Shortly after one of Pearson's labs is raided by amateur MMA fighters and aspiring YouTubers "The Toddlers" who manage to overpower the guards, steal a van-load of cannabis and upload a rap video of their escapade online. The group's trainer is only known as "Coach" and once he hears of their stunt he orders them to delete the video immediatly and is horrified when he discovers that the drugs beling to Pearson.
